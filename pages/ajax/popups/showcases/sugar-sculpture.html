Sugar for the rich

A luxuary to be used by only the privledged

Sugar in the Early Modern Period was very much a “symbol of status” (BBC Worldwide 1:51), and was often used as a medium for arts, such as sculpture. Those outside of the palace ate food to sustain themselves, but those within the royal court used sugar to help illuminate their lavish lifestyle, adding decadence to their food and showing off.
